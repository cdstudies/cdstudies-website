## 4. Copywriting Style

### Voice & Tone
- **First person plural:** Consistently "we" and "our" — creates shared mission feeling
- **Empathetic and warm:** Never clinical, legalistic, or confrontational
- **Deliberately non-political:** Frames the issue as a community/support failure, not a political debate
- **Story-driven:** The founder's mother's story is the emotional anchor
- **Aspirational:** Forward-looking language about transformation and culture change
- **Innovation/startup vocabulary:** "innovation lab," "blue ocean," "scaling," "momentum," "movements"

### Primary Tagline
> **"Making Motherhood *Thinkable*"**

"Thinkable" is always italicized — it's their signature linguistic device. An unusual word choice that becomes the brand's verbal identity.

### Homepage Messaging Hierarchy
This is the sequence of how they build their argument:

1. **Hero headline:** "Making Motherhood *thinkable* for everyone."
2. **Problem (statistics):** "Every year, more than one million women get abortions for one reason: They feel like they have no other choice."
3. **Insight (the reframe):** "Lacking support from their communities, the loudest cultural voices tell her that she must choose between her child and her bright future — not both."
4. **Core belief:** "We believe that the only way to truly create a culture of Life is by launching movements that transform communities and institutions from the inside out."
5. **Vision (aspirational):** "In just one generation, we aim for all women — no matter her circumstances — to receive complete support..."
6. **Areas of work:** Workplaces, Medical Education, Online, Faith Communities
7. **Culminating reframe:** "Abortion is a failure of community."

### Key Copywriting Techniques

**Reframing:** The single most important technique. They consistently reframe abortion as a community/support failure rather than an individual moral choice. This is the strategic core of their messaging.

**"Double-win" framing:** Prosperity for BOTH mother and baby — not a zero-sum sacrifice. "She must choose between her child and her bright future — not both."

**Emotional progression:** Statistics → empathy → insight → vision → action. They don't lead with the ask; they build the case emotionally first.

**Bold/italic emphasis:** Key phrases are visually emphasized: *no other choice*, *launching movements*, *innovation lab*, *thinkable*

**Coined terminology:** "Making motherhood thinkable" is not standard language. Creating distinctive phrases makes the brand memorable and quotable.

**Absent vocabulary:** Notably avoids "pro-life," "pro-choice," or any partisan language. "Culture of Life" appears once. This broadens appeal beyond the traditional base.

### Founder Origin Story (About Page)
The founder (Josiah Friedman) tells the story of his mother's difficult circumstances. Key quote: "Her story broke my heart and it changed me." This personal narrative serves as the emotional foundation for the entire organization.

## 5. Marketing Strategy

### Calls to Action
| CTA | Location | Purpose |
|-----|----------|---------|
| Donate | Primary nav (always visible) | Revenue |
| Learn More | Homepage sections | Engagement |
| Follow the Movement | Multiple pages (newsletter) | Email capture |
| Join the Oak Collective | Oak Collective page | Major donor cultivation |
| Discover the Innovation Lab | About page | Deeper engagement |

### Donation Flow
- **Online:** Stripe integration via WP Simple Pay Pro
  - Custom amount entry (starting at $1.00)
  - Recurring donation checkbox
  - Optional memo field
  - ACH transfer option
- **Offline:** Check payment with mailed instructions
- **Tax info:** "All donations are 100% tax-deductible" + Tax ID prominently displayed
- **Trust signals:** Guidestar profile link

### Giving Tiers

**General donations:** Any amount, one-time or recurring.

**Oak Collective** (major donor society): $2,023+/year or $170/month
- Annual impact report
- Insider updates and prayer requests
- Exclusive annual "Oak Collective Jubilee" event
- Member pin + annual t-shirt
- Positioned for "movement leaders, mission partners, and prayer warriors"

Two member testimonials on the Oak Collective page provide social proof (Kim M. and Jan O.).

### Newsletter/Email Capture
- "Follow the Movement" section appears on homepage, about page, and donate page
- Copy: "Get updates on our movement and learn how you can help"
- Simple email signup — low friction

### Social Proof Elements
- 300+ in-depth interviews (research credibility)
- TED Talk reference (authority)
- Guidestar profile (financial transparency)
- Oak Collective member testimonials
- Innovation lab framing borrows startup credibility

### What's Missing
- No social media links anywhere on the site
- No blog or content marketing
- No impact metrics dashboard
- No team page with bios
- No partner logos or media mentions
- No event calendar
- No volunteer signup
- No resource library

| Audience | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| Mid-to-high level donors | Oak Collective at $2,023+/year; "invest" language |
| Faith-adjacent (not exclusively religious) | "Prayer warriors," faith communities as a focus area, but subtle |

| People disillusioned with political approaches | Explicit pivot away from "political realm" and debate |
| Next-generation leaders | "Visionary next-generation leaders" language |
| Women and families | Imagery, support-centered messaging |

The business/startup vocabulary targets younger, entrepreneurial donors. The avoidance of partisan language broadens appeal beyond the traditional pro-life base.

**Vision:** "In just one generation, we aim for all women — no matter her circumstances — to receive complete support from their doctors, employers, faith communities, families, partners, and even the internet on their motherhood journey."

## 8. Transferable Patterns for CDS

### Adopt
- **Minimal site structure.** VFTV proves that 4–5 pages is enough for a nonprofit launch. CDS can start with: Home, About, Donate, Contact.
- **Messaging hierarchy pattern.** Lead with the problem + urgency, provide insight/reframe, cast vision, then ask. Don't lead with the donation ask.
- **Earthy/warm color palette.** Avoids looking political or generic. CDS should similarly choose a distinctive palette that signals warmth and seriousness without partisanship.
- **Serif + sans-serif font pairing.** Elegant headings + friendly body text strikes the right balance for a serious cause that wants to be approachable.
- **"Donate" in primary nav.** Always visible, never buried.
- **Footer newsletter signup on every page.** Low-friction email capture.
- **Tax-deductible messaging on donate page.** 501(c)(3) status, Tax ID, and "100% tax-deductible" prominently displayed.
- **Stripe for payment processing.** Clean, trusted, supports recurring donations.
- **Founder's personal story as emotional anchor.** If CDS has a founder story, it should be central.

### Adapt
- **The "reframing" technique.** VFTV reframes abortion as "a failure of community." CDS should similarly reframe depopulation — not as a political issue but as a civilizational challenge that transcends politics. Find your version of "Making Motherhood Thinkable."
- **Innovation/startup language.** VFTV uses "innovation lab," "blue ocean," etc. CDS might use "research centre," "demographic intelligence," "policy lab" — academic/research credibility rather than startup credibility.
- **Major donor society.** The Oak Collective model works but CDS may want different naming, pricing, and benefits. The concept of a named insider circle with exclusive access is worth keeping.
- **Non-political positioning.** VFTV avoids "pro-life" language. CDS should similarly avoid language that triggers partisan reactions — frame as data-driven, evidence-based, civilization-level concern.
- **Aspirational imagery.** VFTV uses mothers and children. CDS should find imagery that evokes the positive vision (thriving families, children, future generations) rather than doom.

### Avoid
- **No social media links.** This seems like an oversight on VFTV's part. CDS should include social links even if accounts are minimal.
- **No team page.** For CDS, showing researchers/advisors adds credibility. A team or advisory board page matters for an organization positioning itself as a "centre for studies."
- **No blog/content.** CDS should plan for a research/insights section — it's a "studies" organization, so publishing content is core to credibility.
- **WordPress/block editor.** CDS is already set up with a modern stack (likely Next.js + shadcn/ui), which will give much better performance and design flexibility.

---

## 9. Key Takeaways

1. **Find your "thinkable."** CDS needs a signature phrase that reframes depopulation in a way that's fresh, non-partisan, and emotionally resonant.
2. **Story first, data second.** The emotional narrative opens the door; the statistics validate it.
3. **Less is more.** Launch with fewer pages, each one polished. You can always add more.
4. **Color and type do heavy lifting.** VFTV's earthy palette instantly communicates "we're not a typical political org." CDS's palette should do the same work.
5. **The donation flow should be frictionless.** Stripe, custom amounts, recurring option, check alternative. Keep it simple.
6. **Position as post-partisan.** Both VFTV and CDS benefit from avoiding tribal language and instead appealing to shared human values.
